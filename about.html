Picogrid's mission is to provide real-time access to the most remote corners of planet Earth.

Picogrid was founded to solve one of the most difficult challenges of remote fieldwork: access to reliable power and connectivity. From anti-poaching operations in East Africa to remote wildfire cameras in Northern California to oil pipeline early-leak detection, fieldwork is often limited by the lack of access to real-time, actionable data from field sensors. Further, limited internet access has held remote operations back from taking advantage of the rise of cloud analytics.

Picogrid's history can be traced to a project funded by the US Department of Defense's Environmental Security Technology Certification Program (ESTCP) at the Barry M. Goldwater Air Force Range (BMGR), a remote and very rugged military training facility in the Sonoran Desert of Southern Arizona. Air Force Range Management personnel would spend days manually collecting photos camera traps spread throughout the range.

Our prototypes enabled the biologists and ecologists of the Air Force Range Management Office to more efficiently collect and analyze photos of dozens of species including the endagered Sonoran pronghorn and desert bighorn sheep. Ongoing efforts of the Air Force Range Mangement at BMGR have enabled a recolonization of the area by species which had nearly gone extinct in the region due to dought, livestock overgrazing, and habitat loss.

Early prototypes developed alongside the US Department of Defense formed the basis for Picogrid's Lander, enabling access to field sensors and equipment in any environment, no matter how remote. Our technology continues to be built to military standards and used in some of the most rugged, dangerous, and remote locations around the world.
